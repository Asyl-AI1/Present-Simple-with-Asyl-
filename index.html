What is the Present Simple?

We use the Present Simple to talk about things that are habits or routines, facts, and things we like or dislike. It's the tense for 'always true' or 'true for a long time'.

Habits & Routines üèê

Likes & Dislikes üé≠

How to Use It

The verb form changes a little bit depending on the subject. Let's see how it works. Click the tabs below to explore!

This is for saying 'yes' sentences.

Subject Example
I / You / We / They I play volleyball.
He / She / It She listens to Adele.

This is for saying 'no' sentences. We use `don't` or `doesn't`.

Subject Example
I / You / We / They I don't play volleyball.
He / She / It She doesn't listen to Adele.

This is for asking questions. We start with `Do` or `Does`.

Subject Example
I / You / We / They Do you play volleyball?
He / She / It Does she listen to Adele?

Remember the Rules! ü§Ø

For **he, she, it**, we add an **-s** to the verb!

  • Verbs ending in **-o, -sh, -ch, -ss, -x, -z**: add **-es** (e.g., watch ‚Üí watches)
  • Verbs ending in **consonant + y**: change **-y** to **-i** and add **-es** (e.g., study ‚Üí studies)

Time Expressions

These words help us show how often something happens. They usually go before the main verb.

always usually often sometimes never every day
